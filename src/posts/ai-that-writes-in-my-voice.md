I am not a developer. I have never shipped a line of code that someone paid for. And a few months ago I built a small AI assistant that writes in my voice, the way I actually talk, instead of the flat, everyone-sounds-the-same way that AI usually does.

I want to tell you how, because the hard part was not the technology. The hard part was the thing I already knew as a brand strategist, and had been avoiding applying to myself.

## The problem with letting AI write for you

If you have ever asked a chatbot to write a caption, you know the feeling. It comes back polished and confident and completely not you. It sounds like a brand deck. It uses words you would never say out loud. So you either post it and feel like a fraud, or you rewrite the whole thing and wonder why you bothered.

That happens because you asked the AI to *be* a voice. And with no better instructions, it reaches for the average of everything it has ever read, which is exactly the beige, promotional tone you were trying to escape.

The fix is to stop asking it to invent a voice, and start giving it yours.

## An assistant is just your own words, organized

Here is the whole secret, and it is almost annoying how simple it is. A useful branding assistant is three things:

1. **Your real voice**, captured from words you have already written.
2. **Clear instructions** about how to use that voice.
3. An AI to run it.

The AI is the easy, replaceable part. What makes the output sound like you is the first two, and those are not a coding problem. They are a *noticing* problem, which, if you make anything at all, you are already good at.

## One prompt to start: find your voice

Before you build anything, you need to know what your voice actually is. Most people cannot describe their own, because it is invisible to them. So let the AI notice it for you.

Gather five to ten things you have genuinely written. Not marketing copy. Real ones: a text to a friend about your work, an old caption you liked, a paragraph from an email where you forgot to be careful. Paste them into any chat AI (I use Claude, but ChatGPT works too) with this:

> Here are things I have written in my own words. Do not rewrite them. Instead, notice the patterns in how I actually talk. Tell me: the words and phrases I reach for, the rhythm of my sentences, what I do that is a little unusual, and the three things that make my writing sound like me and not like generic AI. Quote my own lines back as evidence.

Read what comes back. Most people feel a small jolt, because it is the first time anyone has put words to the thing they do without thinking. That description *is* the beginning of your assistant. Save it.
